Ease Dropped

You think you have #firstworldproblems? These New Yorkers are full of them.

For five days I took out my headphones and actually started paying attention to what the people around me said.
Doing this forced me to get out of my head and listen to what the people around me actually talked about.
Inspired by the Instagram account @OverheardNewYork I wanted to use the cities inhabitants as my muse
to create five different art pieces that they unknowingly dictated to me.

In my deep dive into ease dropping I gave myself two rules to follow
1. Listen to a strangers conversation
2. Take one quote from that conversation and do a one minute sketch of it

Day 1


“… and her makeup was fucked.”
“Who?”
“Nancy.”
“Nancy as in Nancy’s funeral Nancy?”
"Yeah.”

Day 2


“Well I turned on the water and it was brown so thats fun.”

Day 3


“I was so hungover.”
“Did you get some Gatorade or something?”
“No, I just downed two bottles of wine on my couch.”

Day 4


“I can’t believe they’re 50 cents now!”
“Right, like the 35 cents was to much … bananas are not a right anymore!”

Day 5


“… bro, when I got home he had used the last Q-Tip and didn’t even get new Q-tips … what am I supposed to do with dirty ear canals.”

After the completion of this project I found myself paying more attention to what I said when I was on the
phone in public and talking to my friends in places like coffee shops and restaurants. I had spent a week
listening to strangers which made me more conscious of that fact that those strangers could just as easily have been
listening to me. I always felt a sense of privacy within public spaces in New York because everyone has their own agenda
and couldn’t care less about me or what I’m doing but after this project I am more aware that this might not always be true.
